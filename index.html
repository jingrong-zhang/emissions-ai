The Anatomy of Traffic — Fleet Density
Not all vehicles weigh the same inside system.
A small number of heavy-duty vehicles can dominate local emissions. Who moves matters as much as how many.
The Anatomy of Traffic — Signal Rhythm
Congestion is not slow. It is rhythmic.
Traffic signals impose stop–start cycles that concentrate idling and acceleration. These repeated interruptions generate emission spikes invisible to average-speed maps.
The Anatomy of Traffic — Acceleration Roughness
Acceleration is invisible in inventories, but dominant in emissions.
Emissions respond to how unevenly traffic accelerates and decelerates, not to speed alone. Streets moving equally fast can pollute very differently.

AI Emissions
Visualizing the dynamics of urban emissions.
Description
Emissions AI reveals how urban traffic emissions emerge not as static totals, but as dynamic patterns shaped by movement, interruption, and infrastructure. By integrating traffic camera data with AI-driven vehicle recognition and network-scale modeling, the project captures fine-grained variations in fleet composition, signal rhythms, and acceleration behavior. This approach uncovers hidden emission hotspots and temporal spikes that conventional models overlook. As demonstrated in Manhattan, such high-resolution insight enables real-time evaluation of policies—from congestion pricing to demand shifts—transforming emissions from an abstract metric into a visible, actionable layer of the city.
